Touring involves coordinating with a lot of people — booking agents, managers, promoters, venue production staff, and more. Roadcase gives you a centralized contact database so your team always knows who to reach out to and how.

## Contact types

Contacts in Roadcase can represent anyone your team works with on a regular basis:

- **Booking agents** who route your tours
- **Managers** and business contacts
- **Venue contacts** like talent buyers, production managers, and box office staff
- **Promoters** and local production reps
- **Label contacts**, publicists, and other industry professionals

## Adding contacts

You can add contacts in two ways:

1. **From a venue** — When editing a venue, add contacts directly to that venue's contact list. These contacts are automatically available when you book a show at that venue.
2. **Inline during advancing** — When preparing an advance for a show, you can add new contacts on the fly.

### Contact fields

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Name** | The contact's full name |
| **Role** | Their role or title (e.g., "Talent Buyer", "Production Manager") |
| **Email** | Email address for correspondence |
| **Phone** | Phone number |

## Venue-linked contacts

Contacts associated with a venue carry over to every show at that venue. This means once you have added a venue's talent buyer or production manager, that information is ready to go the next time you play the same room.

<Tip>
  When you advance a show, Roadcase makes venue contacts available for sharing advance details. Build out your venue contacts over time and you will spend less effort tracking down the right person for each show.
</Tip>

## Using contacts in advances

When you share an [Advance](/show-operations/advances) for a show, you can select from the venue's contacts to send advance information to. This streamlines the advance process and ensures the right people receive the technical and logistical details they need.
