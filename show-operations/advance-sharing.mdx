Advancing a show means sending your technical and hospitality requirements to the venue before the performance. Roadcase makes this effortless by generating a secure, shareable link that gives venue contacts access to your full [advance](/show-operations/advances) package without needing a Roadcase account.

## How sharing works

When your advance is ready to send:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the show advance">
    Navigate to the show and open the **Advance** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Share advance">
    Click the **Share advance** button to open the sharing dialog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select venue contacts">
    Choose which venue contacts should receive the advance. Contacts are pulled from the [venue's contact list](/tour-planning/venues).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send">
    Roadcase generates a tokenized link and emails it to the selected contacts.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What the venue sees

The shared advance is a public page — no login required. The recipient sees a professionally formatted page with:

- Your artist logo and show details
- [Technical rider](/show-operations/riders) with full formatting
- [Hospitality rider](/show-operations/riders) with full formatting
- [Stage plot](/show-operations/stage-plots) with visual layout and input channel list
- Downloadable documents (contracts, production schedules, parking maps, etc.)

This gives the venue everything they need to prepare for your show in one place.

## Tracking engagement

Roadcase tracks how venue contacts interact with your shared advance:

| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| **Page views** | How many times the advance page has been viewed |
| **Document downloads** | Which attached documents have been downloaded and when |
| **Viewer info** | IP address and user agent of viewers |

This gives you confidence that the venue has actually reviewed your requirements. If the advance has not been viewed as show day approaches, you know to follow up.

## Managing shared links

| Action | Description |
|---|---|
| **Resend** | Send the advance email again to the same contacts |
| **Revoke** | Disable the shared link so it no longer works |
| **Content change detection** | Roadcase flags if advance content has changed since it was last shared |

<Note>
  Shared advance links automatically expire **7 days after the show date**. This ensures your technical information is not accessible indefinitely.
</Note>

## When to re-share

If you update your advance after sharing — for example, updating the stage plot or adding a document — Roadcase alerts you that the shared version is out of date. You can then re-share to send the updated advance to the venue.

<Tip>
  Get your advance out to venues as early as possible, ideally two to four weeks before the show. This gives the venue time to review your requirements and flag any issues.
</Tip>
