Per diems are daily allowances paid to band members and crew to cover meals and incidental expenses while on tour. Roadcase lets you configure per diem rates, track who receives them, and automatically factor the total into your show settlements.

## Setting up per diems

Per diem rates are configured at the artist level:

1. Navigate to **Settings > Per diems**
2. Set the **daily rate** (e.g., $30 per person per day)
3. Choose which team members receive per diems

You can set different rates based on team member roles if needed. For example, crew members might receive a different daily rate than band members.

## How per diems appear in settlements

When you run a [settlement](/show-operations/settlements) for a show, Roadcase calculates the total per diem cost based on:

- The daily rate per person
- The number of eligible team members attending the show
- The number of days (for multi-day events)

This total appears as a line item in the settlement, giving you a clear picture of the per diem expense alongside other show costs.

| Settlement field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Per diem rate** | The daily amount per person |
| **Eligible members** | How many team members are receiving per diems for this show |
| **Total per diems** | Rate multiplied by eligible members multiplied by days |

## Per diems and event attendance

Per diem calculations use [event attendance](/show-operations/event-attendance) to determine who is present at each show. Mark team members as attending to include them in the per diem count, or mark them as absent to exclude them.

<Tip>
  Keep your event attendance up to date so that per diem totals in settlements are accurate. If a crew member is not on a particular run, marking them absent ensures they are not included in the calculation.
</Tip>

## Tracking per diems over time

Per diem expenses accumulate across a tour. By reviewing settlements for a date range, you can see total per diem costs for an entire run, making it straightforward to reconcile per diem spending during tour accounting.
