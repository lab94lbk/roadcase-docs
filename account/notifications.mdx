title: Notifications

Roadcase has a multi-channel notification system that keeps your team informed about changes without overwhelming them. You control exactly what notifications you receive and through which channels.

## Notification channels

| Channel | Description |
|---|---|
| **In-app inbox** | Notifications appear in your Roadcase inbox, accessible from the bell icon |
| **Email** | Notifications are sent to your registered email address |
| **Push** | Notifications are sent to your mobile device (iOS and Android) |

## Notification types

Roadcase sends notifications for these events:

| Event | Description |
|---|---|
| **Event created** | A new event is added to the calendar |
| **Event updated** | An event's details change (date, time, venue, etc.) |
| **Event deleted** | An event is removed from the calendar |
| **Schedule change** | A show's schedule items are updated |
| **Venue change** | A show's venue is changed |
| **Todo assigned** | A todo is assigned to you |
| **Guest list request** | A new guest list request is submitted (admin only) |
| **Invitation accepted** | A team invitation is accepted |

## Configuring preferences

You can control notifications at a granular level:

1. Navigate to **Settings > Notifications**
2. For each notification type, toggle which channels are active:
   - In-app inbox: on/off
   - Email: on/off
   - Push: on/off

### Per-artist preferences

If you belong to multiple artists, you can configure notification preferences independently for each artist. This lets you stay closely informed about one artist while reducing noise from another.

## Batched notifications

Roadcase intelligently batches rapid changes to avoid notification overload. For example, if an administrator makes several quick edits to a show's schedule, you receive a single notification summarizing the changes rather than one notification per edit.

The batching window is short enough that you stay informed in near real-time, but long enough to collapse rapid successive edits.

## Push notifications

To receive push notifications on your mobile device:

1. Install the Roadcase [mobile app](/account/mobile-app)
2. Sign in to your account
3. Grant notification permissions when prompted
4. Your device is automatically registered for push notifications

Push notifications are delivered via Apple Push Notification Service (APNs) for iOS and Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) for Android.

### Managing push devices

You can have push notifications active on multiple devices simultaneously. Each device is registered independently and can be managed from your notification settings.

## Email notifications

Email notifications are sent from Roadcase to your registered email address. They are delivered reliably and include enough context that you can understand the change without opening the app.

To reduce email volume, adjust your per-notification-type preferences to disable email for lower-priority events.
