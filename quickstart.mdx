title: Quickstart

---

Get up and running with Roadcase in five steps. The entire process takes less than five minutes.

## 1. Create your account

Roadcase uses passwordless authentication. Enter your email address on the sign-in page and you will receive a magic link. Click the link to sign in — no password required.

Your first sign-in automatically creates your Roadcase account. Every future sign-in works the same way: enter your email, click the link.

## 2. Set up your first artist

After signing in for the first time, you will be prompted to create your first artist. This is the band, solo project, or act that you want to manage.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter your artist name">
    This is the name that appears across Roadcase — on your calendar, in show details, and on any documents you generate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose an avatar color">
    Pick from 16 color options. This color identifies your artist throughout the interface, which is especially useful if you manage multiple artists.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload a logo (optional)">
    Your logo appears on generated PDFs like riders, stage plots, and settlements. You can add or change this later under artist settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## 3. Invite your team

Navigate to your artist's team settings to invite members and crew. Each person gets an email invitation with a magic link to join.

When inviting someone, you assign their role:

| Role | Best for |
|---|---|
| **Administrator** | Band leaders, managers, booking agents — full access to everything |
| **Member** | Musicians and performers — calendar access, availability, guest lists |
| **Crew** | Tour managers, engineers, techs — logistics access, attendance, expenses |

For members, you can also set their instrument (guitar, drums, vocals, etc.). For crew, you set their crew role (tour manager, sound engineer, lighting tech, etc.).

See [Roles & permissions](/core-concepts/roles-and-permissions) for a detailed breakdown of what each role can access.

## 4. Create your first show

Head to the calendar and click **New event** to create your first show.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set the date and time">
    Pick when and where the show is happening.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a venue">
    Search for an existing venue or create a new one. Roadcase integrates with Google Places to pull in venue photos and details automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build the schedule">
    Add schedule items like soundcheck, doors, opener set, and headliner set with their times.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## 5. Explore the dashboard

Your dashboard is your home base. It shows upcoming events, pending todos, and — if you are an admin — alerts for things like pending guest list requests, availability conflicts, and outstanding invitations.
