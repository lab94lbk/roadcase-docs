Roadcase walks you through a guided onboarding wizard when you first sign up. The entire process takes less than five minutes.

## Create your account

1. Visit the Roadcase sign-up page
2. Choose your path:
   - **I'm in a band** — For individual musicians and band leaders
   - **I manage multiple bands** — For managers, labels, and agencies (creates an [organization](/core-concepts/organizations) for you)
3. Enter your name and email address
4. Check your inbox for a magic link and click it to sign in

Roadcase uses passwordless authentication — no password required. Every future sign-in works the same way: enter your email, click the link.

## Onboarding wizard

After your first sign-in, the onboarding wizard guides you through three steps.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set up your artist">
    Create your first artist — the band, solo act, or project you want to manage.

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Band / artist name** | The name that appears across Roadcase (required) |
    | **Bio** | A short description of your music and story (optional) |
    | **Profile picture** | Upload a photo for your artist profile (optional, max 25 MB) |
    | **Avatar color** | Pick from 16 colors to identify your artist in the interface |

    If you chose "I manage multiple bands" at sign-up, this artist is automatically linked to your organization.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Schedule your first show">
    Add an upcoming show to get your calendar started.

    | Field | Description |
    |---|---|
    | **Date** | Pick a future date from the calendar |
    | **Venue** | Search for a venue or address — Roadcase searches real places worldwide and auto-fills location details |

    This step is optional. Click **Skip for now** if you do not have a show to add yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You're all set">
    Review a summary of what you just created and click **Take me to Roadcase** to enter the app.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Welcome tour

After completing onboarding, Roadcase shows a brief guided tour that highlights the key parts of the interface:

1. **Your calendar** — The weekly view where all your events appear
2. **Create events** — How to add shows, meetings, and other events (plus the `Cmd+K` command palette)
3. **Team management** — Where to invite members and manage roles
4. **Artist settings** — Where to update your profile, branding, and EPK

The tour appears once and can be dismissed at any time.

## Invite your team

Once you are in the app, navigate to your artist's team settings to invite members and crew. Each person gets an email invitation with a magic link to join.

When inviting someone, you assign their role:

| Role | Best for |
|---|---|
| **Administrator** | Band leaders, managers, booking agents — full access to everything |
| **Member** | Musicians and performers — calendar access, availability |
| **Crew** | Sound engineers, techs, merch — day-of operations access |

For members, you can also set their instrument (guitar, drums, vocals, etc.). For crew, you set their crew role (sound engineer, lighting tech, merchandise, etc.).

<Tip>
  Tour managers typically need administrator access so they can manage shows, deals, settlements, and advances on behalf of the artist.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  People who are invited to an artist before they sign up for Roadcase skip the onboarding wizard entirely — they go straight to the dashboard when they accept the invitation.
</Tip>

See [Roles & permissions](/core-concepts/roles-and-permissions) for a detailed breakdown of what each role can access.
