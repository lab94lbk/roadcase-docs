title: Google Calendar sync
description: Sync your Roadcase events to your personal Google Calendar for a unified view of your schedule.
---

Google Calendar sync lets you connect your personal Google Calendar to any artist you belong to. Your Roadcase events appear automatically in Google Calendar, keeping your personal and professional schedules in one place.

## How it works

Each team member creates their own sync connection. You choose which artist to sync and which event types to include. Once connected, Roadcase pushes events to your Google Calendar in near real-time.

| Sync direction | Who | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| **Roadcase to Google** | Everyone | Events created, updated, or deleted in Roadcase are automatically reflected in your Google Calendar |
| **Google to Roadcase** | Administrators only | Changes made to synced events in Google Calendar (such as rescheduling) are written back to Roadcase |

<Info>
  Non-administrators get a one-way sync. Events appear in your Google Calendar, but editing them in Google does not affect Roadcase.
</Info>

## Setting up a connection

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the sync dialog">
    On the calendar page, click the **Sync** button in the toolbar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose an artist">
    Select the artist whose events you want to sync from the dropdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect with Google">
    Click **Connect**. You will be redirected to Google to authorize Roadcase to access your calendar. Grant the requested permissions and you will be returned to Roadcase.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure event types">
    After connecting, expand the connection to choose which event types to sync. By default, all supported types are enabled.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once connected, Roadcase runs an initial sync to push your existing events to Google Calendar. New events sync automatically going forward.

## Supported event types

You can choose which event types are included in the sync:

| Event type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Show** | Performances at venues |
| **Travel** | Flights, drives, and other travel |
| **Meeting** | Business meetings |
| **Interview** | Press and media interviews |
| **Writing session** | Songwriting sessions |
| **Rehearsal** | Band rehearsals |
| **Hold** | Tentative date holds |
| **Custom event** | Any custom event types you have created |

Use the event type toggles in the sync dialog to enable or disable individual types. Changes take effect immediately.

## Managing connections

### Enable or disable sync

Use the toggle switch next to each connection to pause or resume syncing without disconnecting. When disabled, no new changes are pushed to Google Calendar.

### Disconnect

To remove a connection entirely, expand the connection and click **Disconnect**. This revokes Roadcase's access to your Google Calendar. Events that were already synced remain in Google Calendar but are no longer linked to Roadcase.

## Two-way sync for administrators

If you are an [administrator](/core-concepts/roles-and-permissions) for the connected artist, your sync is two-way. This means:

- **Time changes** you make to synced events in Google Calendar are applied in Roadcase
- **Title changes** you make in Google Calendar are reflected in Roadcase
- **Deleted events** in Google Calendar are unlinked from the sync but not deleted in Roadcase

<Tip>
  Two-way sync is useful when you need to quickly reschedule an event from your phone using Google Calendar. The change flows back to Roadcase within a few minutes.
</Tip>

### What does not sync back

- Creating a new event in Google Calendar does not create one in Roadcase. Events must be created in Roadcase.
- Changes to the event description or location in Google Calendar are not written back.
- Deleting an event in Google Calendar does not delete it in Roadcase — it only removes the sync link.

## One connection per artist

Each team member can have one Google Calendar sync connection per artist. If you belong to multiple artists, you can create a separate connection for each one, potentially using different Google accounts or calendars.

## How events appear in Google Calendar

Synced events appear in your Google Calendar with:

- The event title followed by the artist name (for example, "Madison Square Garden (The Example Band)")
- The venue or location, if available
- A description with the event type and any notes

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I sync to a shared Google Calendar instead of my personal one?**
Events sync to your primary Google Calendar by default.

**What happens if I lose access to the artist?**
If your membership is removed, the sync connection is automatically deleted. Events already in Google Calendar remain but are no longer updated.

**How quickly do changes sync?**
Changes from Roadcase to Google Calendar happen within seconds. Changes from Google Calendar to Roadcase (for administrators) are picked up within a few minutes.
